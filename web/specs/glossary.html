# Glossary

Affiliate - used on EAP side. Acting on behalf of Quantum. Quantum is responsible and assigns them. In the new software, they are also "providers", whereas in Daybreak they used to be a completely separate type of record

Client - those who are receiving treatment. May be employees of a company or a dependent (family member).

Daybreak - Current EAP software Quantum is using that we will be replacing

Dependent - an immediate family member of an employee. See "clients"

Employers - Organizations that contract with Quantum to offer services for their employees

Employee - a client that works for the employer company. See "client"

EAP - Employee Assistance Program - managed by Quantum, but services are paid by third party insurance companies like blue cross blue shield. Services are limited, this is not a medical benefit.

"An employee assistance program (EAP) is a work-based intervention program designed to assist employees in resolving personal problems that may be adversely affecting the employee's performance. EAPs traditionally have assisted workers with issues like alcohol or substance abuse; however, most now cover a broad range of issues such as child or elder care, relationship challenges, financial or legal problems, wellness matters and traumatic events like workplace violence. Programs are delivered at no cost to employees by stand-alone EAP vendors or providers who are part of comprehensive health insurance plans. Services are often delivered via phone, video-based counseling, online chatting, e-mail interactions or face-to-face."

EOB - Explanation of Benefits (in Managed Care) - a report provided back to providers and employers

EOP Explanation of Payment (same as EOB, but on the EAP side)

Facilities - a group of providers offering services to patients. Sometimes providers are part of and would better be described as an "organization", "clinic" etc., but for the purposes of this software we are managing groups of providers as facilities.

Indecs - a third party company that is cutting checks in the EAP

Managed Care - As EAP, but Quantum pays for services. Companies hire quantum to be the direct contact for employees. Quantum establishes contact to providers, handles authorizations and claims. This is a medical benefit plan for behavioural health.

OTR - Ongoing Treatment Report - only used on EAP side, not Managed Care.

Provider - used on managed care side. Quantum has no involvement in treatment, only monitoring it, case managing from a plan perspective. Ensuring provider is following protocol. A list of providers is given to clients for them to choose.
